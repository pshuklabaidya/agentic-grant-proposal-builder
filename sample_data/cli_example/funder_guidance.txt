Youth Opportunity Grant Program.

Eligible applicants include nonprofit community-based organizations serving low-income youth and families. Priority will be given to programs that improve academic persistence, career readiness, mentoring access, family engagement, measurable outcomes, and sustainable community partnerships.

Required proposal sections include a need statement, target population description, project design, implementation timeline, staffing plan, budget narrative, evaluation plan, expected outcomes, sustainability strategy, and organizational capacity.

Allowable costs include personnel, curriculum, participant support, transportation assistance, technology, evaluation, outreach materials, and reasonable indirect costs. Applicants should include measurable outputs, outcomes, data collection methods, reporting cadence, and a clear description of how grant funds will support direct services.
