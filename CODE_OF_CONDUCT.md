# Code Of Conduct

## Purpose

Agentic Grant Proposal Builder is a portfolio project for demonstrating applied AI engineering, Agentic RAG, OpenAI tool-calling agents, Streamlit dashboards, deterministic fallback behavior, quality gates, and evaluation reports.

This code of conduct sets expectations for respectful participation in issues, pull requests, discussions, reviews, and related project communication.

## Expected Behavior

Participants are expected to:

- Use welcoming and respectful language
- Stay focused on the project and technical substance
- Give constructive feedback
- Respect differing levels of experience
- Avoid personal attacks, harassment, and inflammatory remarks
- Avoid sharing secrets, private grant documents, private applicant data, or confidential materials
- Preserve the project safety boundary around human review and non-guaranteed grant outcomes

## Unacceptable Behavior

Unacceptable behavior includes:

- Harassment, intimidation, or abusive language
- Personal attacks or demeaning comments
- Publishing private information without permission
- Sharing secrets, tokens, private applicant data, or confidential grant materials
- Encouraging claims of guaranteed eligibility, compliance, award likelihood, or funder acceptance
- Submitting malicious code, credential-harvesting changes, or unsafe automation

## AI-Specific Expectations

Contributions should preserve the following boundaries:

- Generated proposal content must remain reviewable by humans.
- The app must not claim final legal, financial, or funder compliance.
- The app must not claim guaranteed award likelihood.
- Secrets must not be printed, logged, committed, or exposed in the UI.
- Private grant materials should not be added to the public repository.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers may remove comments, close issues, reject pull requests, or block participation when conduct harms the project, exposes secrets, or violates the safety boundaries of the application.

## Reporting

Report issues through GitHub issues when appropriate.

Do not include secrets, API keys, access tokens, private grant documents, private applicant data, or confidential materials in a public issue.
